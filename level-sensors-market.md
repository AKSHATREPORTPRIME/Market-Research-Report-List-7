<p><p>The level sensors market is categorized into three primary types: for liquids, for solids, and others. Liquid level sensors measure the height of liquids in tanks or reservoirs, utilizing technologies like ultrasonic, capacitive, or float-based systems. Solid level sensors detect the presence and level of solid materials, often employing technologies such as vibratory or radar sensors. The "others" category encompasses niche applications, including slurry and paste measurement, catering to specific industry needs like food processing or chemical manufacturing.</p></p>
