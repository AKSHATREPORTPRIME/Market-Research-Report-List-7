<p><p>A Dual Wavelength Selective Switch (WSS) is a crucial component in optical networking, allowing for the selective routing of signals across different wavelengths. The market for WSS technology is primarily divided into two types: LCOS (Liquid Crystal on Silicon) based and MEMS (Micro-Electro-Mechanical Systems) based solutions. LCOS-based WSS offers high resolution and low insertion loss, making it suitable for dense wavelength division multiplexing. In contrast, MEMS-based WSS provides faster switching times and reliability, catering to applications requiring quick signal adjustments.</p></p>
