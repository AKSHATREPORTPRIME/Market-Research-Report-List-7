<p><p>Self-supported warehouses are categorized into various market types based on storage configurations. Single deep warehouses feature straightforward, single-row shelving, allowing easy access but lower density. Double deep warehouses maximize space by stacking pallets two-deep, increasing storage capacity at the expense of accessibility. Other configurations may include drive-in or multi-level systems, optimizing vertical space and facilitating bulk storage. Each type caters to specific operational needs, balancing accessibility, density, and efficient inventory management according to the warehouse's purpose.</p></p>
