<p><p>The conductive modified plastics market comprises various types, primarily structural conductive plastics and composite conductive plastics. Structural conductive plastics are engineered to provide both mechanical strength and electrical conductivity, making them ideal for applications requiring robust, electrically conductive components. On the other hand, composite conductive plastics integrate conductive fillers like carbon or metal particles into a polymer matrix, enhancing conductivity while maintaining lightweight and versatile properties. Both types serve industries such as electronics, automotive, and aerospace, addressing diverse functional requirements.</p></p>
