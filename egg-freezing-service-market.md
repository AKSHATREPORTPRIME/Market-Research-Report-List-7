<p><ul><li>Slow Freezing</li><li>Rapid Freezing</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The egg freezing service market primarily consists of two techniques: slow freezing and rapid freezing. Slow freezing, also known as traditional cryopreservation, gradually cools eggs to optimize cell integrity, but it can lead to ice crystal formation. In contrast, rapid freezing, or vitrification, involves an ultra-fast cooling process that prevents ice crystals from forming, enhancing survival rates during thawing. Both methods cater to women seeking to preserve fertility for personal or medical reasons, shaping their reproductive choices.</p></p>
