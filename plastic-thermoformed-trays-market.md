<p><p>The plastic thermoformed trays market includes various types of materials, each with unique properties. PP (Polypropylene) offers durability and heat resistance, ideal for food packaging. PET (Polyethylene Terephthalate) is lightweight, transparent, and recyclable, commonly used for beverages and food. HDPE (High-Density Polyethylene) provides strength and moisture resistance, suitable for heavy-duty applications. HIPS (High-Impact Polystyrene) is cost-effective and impact-resistant, often used for disposable trays. PVC (Polyvinyl Chloride) is flexible and chemical-resistant, used in medical and industrial packaging. LDPE (Low-Density Polyethylene) is soft and flexible, ideal for lightweight applications. ABS (Acrylonitrile Butadiene Styrene) offers toughness and heat resistance, used in electronics and automotive. Other materials cater to niche applications.</p></p>
